Frequently Asked Questions

Deadline to nominate: August 15, 2026

About the Program

What are Federal Opportunity Zones? ▼

Federal Opportunity Zones are low-income census tracts identified by U.S. Treasury and designated by the governor of each state. Federal Opportunity Zones are central to a federal tax incentive program that aims to attract investors to invest capital gains in targeted community development. In return for opportunity zone investments made through Qualified Opportunity Funds (QOFs), investors can reduce their federal tax liability.

What does "Opportunity Zones 2.0" mean? ▼

This program was launched in 2017 through the Tax Cuts and Jobs Act. An original round of opportunity zone designations was made at that time. Through recent legislation — HR 1, or the Big Beautiful Bill — Opportunity Zones were made permanent with some enhancements based on lessons learned. "Opportunity Zones 2.0" refers to the next round of opportunity zone designations, which will subsequently occur every 10 years.

NOTE: Newly designated opportunity zones will take effect January 1, 2027. Only investments made in these zones after that date will qualify for federal tax benefits. Existing opportunity zones (those designated in 2017/2018) will expire December 31, 2026.
How are Opportunity Zones determined? How many does Georgia have? ▼

Congress identified criteria — specifically, poverty rate and household income — to determine census tracts eligible for an opportunity zone designation. The US Treasury identified 942 of Georgia's 2,796 census tracts as eligible to be designated. The state may designate no more than 25% of eligible tracts as opportunity zones and is currently evaluating data to determine which 236 census tracts should be designated.

Can I nominate a census tract in my community for designation? What makes a strong nomination? ▼

Yes, you may nominate any eligible census tract for consideration. Each nomination requires its own form, and there is no cap on the number of nominations.

Quality of nominations is much more significant than quantity. Communities are encouraged to prioritize the strongest nominations and include evidence of both the investment opportunity and local initiatives to attract investment.

While developers are welcome to nominate specific development tracts, this program is designed to be a community development program, not a developer incentive.

Community needs and objectives will factor into considerations just as much as project readiness to proceed.

How will Georgia prioritize Opportunity Zone designations? ▼

By law, not every eligible census tract can be designated as an opportunity zone. Criteria for evaluation will include:

  • Census tract poverty rate and distress indicators
  • Clearly defined community goals and objectives
  • Prior community investment to advance specific goals and objectives, along with planned investment with identified funding sources
  • Alignment with Georgia's economic development priorities
  • Credible projects that are investment ready, including existing financing and/or identified investors
  • Geographic diversity across all regions of the state

Completing the Nomination Form

How do I find the census tract I want to nominate? ▼

On the nomination form, select your county first and the tract list will filter to eligible tracts in that county. If you don't know which tract a specific address falls within, use the "Search by address" link on the Census Tract page of the form, or use the Census Bureau's geocoder at geocoding.geo.census.gov.

What's the difference between the two nomination paths? ▼

Tract investment potential is for nominators who believe a tract deserves OZ designation based on its economic development characteristics, but who do not have a specific project attached. Local governments and EDOs often nominate this way.

Specific project in mind is for nominators who can describe a defined project, business expansion, or development opportunity that OZ designation would directly enable. Both paths are valid. Nominations with specific project detail are generally stronger.

What happens after I submit? ▼

DCA will review all nominations following the August 15, 2026 deadline. Submitting a nomination does not guarantee selection.

  • August 15, 2026 — Georgia nomination deadline
  • September 29, 2026 — Federal deadline for Georgia to submit designations to Treasury
  • January 1, 2027 — OZ 2.0 investment eligibility begins

DCA will notify nominators once selections are finalized.
