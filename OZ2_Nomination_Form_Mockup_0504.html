OZ 1.0 in Georgia: What the Program Delivered

Georgia designated 260 census tracts under OZ 1.0 in 2018. Nationally, the program catalyzed an estimated $100 billion in private investment — spurring over 300,000 housing units and economic activity that extended into neighboring communities. In Georgia, confirmed OZ 1.0 investments include:

Build-to-Rent Housing (Decatur & Augusta Region) — Pinnacle Partners and Trilogy Investment raised $60 million in OZ equity through a 10-year hold structure to develop 45 market-rate townhomes in Decatur and 245 workforce housing units in the Augusta region. OZ equity served as the primary equity layer, not gap fill.

Atlanta-Area Multifamily & Mixed-Use — Atlanta ranked among the top U.S. metros for OZ equity deployment, with over $635 million in OZ equity raised for Atlanta-area projects in 2023 (CoStar). Multiple QOFs tracked by Novogradac identified Georgia as an active investment geography, with a focus on multifamily and workforce housing.

South & Southwest Atlanta Redevelopment (Invest Atlanta) — Invest Atlanta layered OZ equity with local incentives — including tax abatement and gap funding — to move housing and mixed-use redevelopment projects forward in City of Atlanta OZ tracts. Projects moved forward only after OZ equity commitments were secured.
